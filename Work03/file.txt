Education, science and technology, and human resources are the foundational and strategic pillars for building a modern socialist country in all respects. We must regard science and technology as our primary productive force, talent as our primary resource, and innovation as our primary driver of growth. We will fully implement the strategy for invigorating China through science and education, the workforce development strategy, and the innovation-driven development strategy. We will open up new areas and new arenas in development and steadily foster new growth drivers and new strengths. The goal of education is not always to find definitive answers. In fact, if education persistently pursues outcomes, students thinking may become constrained. To avoid this, education should encourage students to think freely, and as long as their thinking is coherent and reasonable, excessive intervention and constraints should not be imposed. Reading is seen by many as a mean to acquire knowledge, but in essence, it is a key that helps students unlock the door to a new world. However, more important than knowledge itself is how to apply it. In the Internet era, knowledge is readily available, but how to combine and piece together this knowledge to generate new value and meaning is the true essence of modern education. At the same time, we should also clarify the difference between education and enterprises. The core of an enterprise is to solve practical problems and meet real-world needs. However, education is not merely about satisfying current demands; more importantly, it aims to cultivate future talents and help them adapt to and shape the world of the future. Lastly, we must clarify the distinction between science and technology. Technology is invented to fulfill specific purposes, but science differs; it exists to explore and discover new knowledge, and to pursue truth. Therefore, genuine scientists often exhibit a passion and perseverance towards the unknown, willing to continuously pursue and explore for a single goal. Education is not merely a process of imparting knowledge, but also a process of cultivating students' independent thinking, innovation, and exploration. We encourage students to pursue the spirit of science and explore unknown fields, because only in this way can we truly cultivate a new generation with forward-thinking and innovative spirit.
